Personal Brand Style Guide

07
🎙️
Voice & Tone
  • Confident, not arrogant. You know your craft — you don't need to prove it.
  • Sharp and direct. No filler words. No "um, so basically..."
  • Slightly edgy. You're willing to say what others in your field won't.
  • Warm when personal. Cold when technical. Switch between both fluidly.
  • Use Tanglish naturally — not forced. Let it emerge in the emotion.
  • Avoid academic jargon. Talk like a smart person explaining to a friend.
📷
Camera Presence
  • Always look directly into the lens. Not above it, not beside it.
  • Stand or sit at eye level. Never shoot from below — it distorts authority.
  • Background: site, desk with drawings, or clean minimal wall. Never random.
  • Clothing: clean, professional, warm tones. Never loud patterns on camera.
  • Hands visible when talking — gestures convey conviction.
  • Lapel mic always. Audio quality is non-negotiable.
✂️
Editing Style
  • Clean jump cuts. No flashy transitions. Restraint = premium.
  • Auto-captions always. Styled in one clean sans-serif, consistently.
  • Background music at -20dB — never louder than your voice.
  • Use text on screen to reinforce key points, not repeat them.
  • Cinematic content: slow, wide shots, architectural close-ups.
  • Pacing: fast for humor and lists. Slow for emotional or conceptual content.

01
Builder Scam Alert
Awareness · High Share

"Bro, un contractor enna solvaan theriyuma? 'Madam, rate same dhaan — just oru chhota sa change.' Adhan problem."


Paarungha — oru plot vaangrom, contractor quotation kudukiran: ₹1800 per sqft. Looks fine.


Construction starts. Two months later: 'Madam, tiles grade change pannuvoama? Little bit costly dhaan.' Month four: 'Flooring rate increase aachu.' Month six: ₹2800 per sqft.


Ithu scam ah theriyala? Because each change — small dhaan. But total? Huge.


Solution enna? BOQ. Bill of Quantities. Material-by-material, work-by-work breakdown. Un architect ku sollu — BOQ illama sign pannama nu. That's it.


→ Comment 'BOQ' below — I'll send you a checklist. Free.

02
Pinterest vs Real Architecture
Myth-Busting · Viral Potential

"Pinterest la oru image save pannittu 'exactly like this' sollitta — architect mathiri feel aagudha? I get it. But listen."


Adhu image Pinterest la beautiful ah irukku. But adhu Chennai climate ku suit aagudha? Theriyala. Adhu un plot size ku fit aagudha? Theriyala. Approvable ah? Definitely theriyala.


AI generate pannadhu — same problem. Render ku nalla irukku. Reality la? Bathroom ventilation illama irukku. Staircase NBC norm follow pannala. Structural wall miss pannirukku.


Naan AI oppose pannalai. But AI + Pinterest is not a design. It's a reference. Design is understanding your site, your climate, your life — and solving it with space.


→ Un dream home ku proper design venum-na — DM pannunga. Let's talk.

03
Climate Design for Tamil Nadu
Educational · Local Relevance

"Un veetu AC 12 hours run aagudha? Architecture blame pannunga — not the weather."


Chennai heat theriyum. But adhu solve aagum — proper design irunthalum.


Oru simple example: Un home east-west orientation la irunthaluma? Morning sun east la — bedroom hot. Evening sun west la — living room hot. AC all day.


North-south orientation la build pannirunthalum? Direct sun exposure kurayum. Cross-ventilation natural ah varum. AC hours half aagum.


Adichu solla mudiyaadha: Good architecture is free AC. Overhang, jaali, courtyard — these are not 'old design.' These are climate solutions that worked 500 years ago and still work today.


→ Un plot orientation pathi doubt irunthalum? Site plan photo podunga comment la — I'll tell you the best orientation. Free.
